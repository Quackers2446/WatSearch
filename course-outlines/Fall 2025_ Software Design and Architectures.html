Course Description

Calendar Description for SE 464

Introduces students to the design, implementation, and evolution phases of software development. Software design processes, methods, and notation. Implementation of designs. Evolution of designs and implementations. Management of design activities.

View requirements for SE 464

This offering follows similar material from fall 2023 which was a revamp to better align with the current state of industry. Particularly, the course talks more about how architecture affects operational quality attributes, such as performance, scalability, robustness, dependability, etc.  

Learning Outcomes

By the end of this course students should be able to:
propose and analyze software architectures.
explain the strengths and weaknesses of various architectural styles and design patterns / techniques.
communicate and rationalize architectural and design decisions.
ideate, justify, and implement software designs.
evaluate, compare, and contrast different architectures and designs.

Notice of Recording

We are fortunate to have some special guest lectures this term. These guest lectures will connect the course material to industry and to research, and will enrich your learning experience.

Guest lectures may be recorded and shared with people outside of class. Questions that you ask of the guest speakers may be recorded. Background conversation that you are having while the guest speaker is speaking might also be accidentally recorded. If you are between the camera and the guest speaker you may appear on video. You will be warned when there is a guest lecturer and it is being recorded. By engaging in course activities that involve recording, you are consenting to the use of your appearance, image, text/chat messaging, and voice and/or likeness in the manner and under the conditions specified herein.

You may choose to not attend the guest lectures and instead watch the recordings afterwards.

Regular lectures and class activities will not be recorded.
